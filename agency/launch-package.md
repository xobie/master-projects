## 3. TARGET NICHE STRATEGY

### Primary Niche (Go Hyper-Specific First)
#### 🎯 HVAC / Air Conditioning Businesses in Greater Sydney

**Why this niche:**
- High call volume (emergencies, seasonal spikes)
- Notorious for missed calls = lost jobs ($500–$2,000 per job)
- Summer/winter peaks create obvious pain points
- Average business: 2–8 techs, overwhelmed owner doing admin
- No-shows and scheduling gaps are endemic
- Strong ROI story: "1 recovered emergency call = pays for the month"
- Sydney has 500+ HVAC/AC businesses; Greater Sydney directory easily sourced
- Trades owners respond to ROI, not tech features — easy pitch

**Pain Points to Hit:**
- "You're losing $3,000–$10,000/month in missed calls when you're on the tools"
- "Your competitor answers at 11pm. Do you?"
- "One missed emergency call in summer = one month's retainer gone"

---

### Backup Niches (Priority Order)

#### 2. Electricians (Solo/Small Teams, Sydney Metro)
- High job ticket ($300–$3,000/job)
- Deeply reactive — calls come in unpredictably
- Quote follow-up is a known revenue leak
- ~3,000 licensed electricians in NSW — targetable database

#### 3. Plumbers (Emergency & Residential)
- Emergency = highest urgency, best use case for 24/7 answering
- "Water's coming through the ceiling" = they need someone NOW
- Plumbers who don't answer lose to whoever answers first
- Strong word-of-mouth referral loop once trust is built

#### 4. Med Spas & Skin Clinics (Inner Sydney / North Shore)
- Higher LTV clients, higher willingness to pay
- Appointment-heavy = scheduling agent is obvious win
- Client follow-up sequences directly drive rebookings
- Affluent owner demographic = less price sensitivity
- Good brand story: "Your clinic runs like a 5-star hotel"

## 7. SALES CALL SCRIPT (4-STAGE ZOOM)

### Pre-Call Setup
- Send calendar invite with Zoom link + short pre-read: "This is a working session, not a sales pitch. We'll map your business."
- Prepare: Review their Google listing, check reviews, note their service area
- Have Loom demo ready + slide deck (optional)

---

### STAGE 1: CONNECT (Minutes 0–5)
**Goal:** Build rapport, disarm, set agenda.

> "Hey [Name], great to meet you. Before we dive in — I want to be straight with you. This is a 30-minute working session. I'm going to ask you some questions about your business, show you where AI can help, and at the end, you decide if it makes sense to go further. No pressure either way. Fair?"

*(Wait for yes)*

> "Perfect. Tell me a bit about [Business Name] — how long have you been running, how many team members, what's a typical week look like for you?"

**Listen actively. Take notes.**

---

### STAGE 2: DIG (Minutes 5–15)
**Goal:** Uncover pain. Let them sell themselves.

Key questions (pick 3–4):

1. "When you're out on a job, what happens when the phone rings?"
2. "How do you handle booking requests that come in after hours?"
3. "What does your week look like admin-wise — how many hours would you say you spend on scheduling, emails, follow-ups?"
4. "Have you ever lost a quote because the follow-up fell through the cracks?"
5. "What would it mean to your business if you never missed a call again?"
6. "If you could magically fix one admin problem tomorrow, what would it be?"

**Bridge question:**
> "So it sounds like the biggest leaks are [X] and [Y]. If we could plug those — how much do you reckon that's worth to you in a year?"

*(Let them say a number. It will anchor the price conversation later.)*

---

### STAGE 3: PRESENT (Minutes 15–25)
**Goal:** Show the solution. Make it concrete. Don't over-explain.

> "Okay — so what I do is build and install a custom AI administrator into your business. It's not a generic chatbot — it's configured specifically for [their business name], your pricing, your services, your clients."

**Walk through the 3 agents:**
- "The first agent handles all incoming calls and messages — answers after hours, books jobs, triages emergencies."
- "The second manages your calendar — prevents double-books, sends reminders, auto-fills gaps."
- "The third handles follow-ups — quote reminders, job updates, client check-ins — all automated."

**Show the dashboard:**
> "You get a dashboard — one screen — where you can see everything. What's been booked, what's been sent, what needs your attention."

**Address the ROI:**
> "You mentioned you're probably losing [number they gave] a week in missed jobs. At your average job ticket, that's roughly $[X] a month. Our retainer is $3,000. So even on a conservative estimate, this pays for itself in the first week."

---

### STAGE 4: CLOSE (Minutes 25–30)
**Goal:** Get a decision or a clear next step.

> "So — based on what we've talked about, does this feel like the right move for [Business Name]?"

*(If yes:)*
> "Great. The way it works: I do a deeper 60-min audit with you first — that's free, no obligation. From there we build the system, takes about 10 days, then we install. You'd be live within 2 weeks. Want to lock in the audit time now?"

*(If uncertain:)*
> "What's the main thing holding you back? Is it timing, budget, or you just want to think it through?"

*(If budget objection:)*
> "Totally get it. Let me ask — what's one new job worth to you? [$X]. And how many jobs do you think you're losing a month to missed calls? So that's [$Y]. Our retainer is $3,000. At that math, you're ahead in week one. Does that change the picture at all?"

*(If not now:)*
> "No worries. Can I send you through a case study from another [trade type] we worked with? And if it makes sense in a month or two, reach back out — this offer will still be here."

## 9. LEGAL & PROTECTION BASICS

*Note: Get a solicitor to review all contracts before use. The below is framework guidance, not legal advice.*

### Business Setup (Australia)
- **ABN:** Register at abr.gov.au (free)
- **Business name:** ASIC business name registration ($39/year) at asic.gov.au
- **GST:** Register if you expect to exceed $75,000/year turnover (likely quickly)
- **Business bank account:** Separate from personal. CBA, NAB, or Macquarie Business work well.
- **Accounting:** Xero ($35–65/mo) or MYOB for invoicing and BAS

### Contracts to Have

#### 1. Service Agreement / Master Service Agreement (MSA)
Key clauses:
- Scope of work (exactly what's included and excluded)
- Payment terms (deposit + milestone + monthly)
- Cancellation: 30-day notice after 3-month minimum
- IP ownership: You own the agent templates; client owns their data
- Limitation of liability: Cap at 3 months' fees
- Acceptable use: Client agrees not to use AI for spam, fraud, or illegal purposes

#### 2. Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA)
- Use before sharing your methodology, tech stack, or agent configs
- Mutual NDA protects both parties

#### 3. Data Processing / Privacy Schedule
- Australia Privacy Act 1988 applies if you handle personal information
- Especially important for med spas (health data = sensitive)
- State clearly: what data you access, how it's stored, who can access it

### Insurance
